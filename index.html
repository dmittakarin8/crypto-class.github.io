1. 001

1.1. Understanding Wallets

1.2. Overview

A cryptocurrency wallet is a digital tool that allows you to store, send, and receive cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, or any other supported digital assets. It’s important to understand that a cryptocurrency wallet doesn’t actually “hold” your coins like a physical wallet holds cash. Instead, it manages your access to the blockchain, where your funds are recorded.

1.2.1. Addresses

An address is a unique identifier on the blockchain where you can send and receive cryptocurrency. It’s like your account number in the cryptocurrency world. Each address is associated with a specific cryptocurrency wallet and has its own balance.

1.2.2. Private Keys

  1. Seed Phrases (Wallet Private Key)

    The wallet private key is the master key that controls the entire wallet. It’s usually generated when you create the wallet and is typically represented by a seed phrase, mnemonic phrase, or a long string of random characters. A seed phrase is like a master key to your crypto wallet. It’s a series of usually 12 to 24 words that serve as a backup for your wallet’s private key.

    Never share this with anyone!

  2. Address Private Key

    Each individual cryptocurrency address within a wallet has its own private key. These address-specific private keys are derived from the wallet private key using cryptographic algorithms. While the wallet private key controls access to all the funds in the wallet, each address private key controls the funds associated specifically with that address.

1.2.3. Seed Phrases -> Addresses

The relationship between seed phrases and private keys is based on a cryptographic concept called hierarchical deterministic wallets (HD wallets).

A seed phrase is the master key that generates a hierarchy of private keys. When you create a wallet using a seed phrase, the wallet uses a specific algorithm called a hierarchical deterministic algorithm to derive a series of private keys from that seed phrase.

Each private key in this hierarchy is unique but is deterministically derived from the seed phrase. This means that if you have the same seed phrase, you can always generate the same sequence of private keys.

Because of this relationship, you can create and manage multiple private keys (and therefore multiple cryptocurrency addresses) using just one seed phrase. This is incredibly useful for managing different accounts or for creating new addresses for each transaction to enhance privacy.

In summary, the seed phrase acts as a single, secure way to generate an unlimited number of private keys, allowing you to access and manage your cryptocurrency across various addresses and accounts.

1.3. Different types of wallets:

1.3.1. Hot wallets (online)

  • seed phrases / keys are stored on your computer
  • good for interacting with unknown apps and for minting ( burner wallets )
  • if your computer is compromised (malware/virus/phishing etc…), your seed phrase could become compromised
  • never store a significant amount of value on a hot wallet

1.3.2. Cold wallets (offline)

  • where long term value should be stored
  • if your computer is compromised it will not affect your cold storage
  • do not used to interact with apps
